## 1. Introduction

The Standard Model contains three independent gauge couplings,
[
g_1,\qquad g_2,\qquad g_3,
]
where
[
g_1^2=\frac53 g_Y^2
]
is the conventional grand-unified normalization of hypercharge. These couplings are among the central empirical inputs of the theory. Gauge symmetry fixes the form of the Yang–Mills interaction, but not the numerical coefficient of
[
-\frac{1}{4g^2}\int F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}.
]
In Wilsonian quantum field theory this coefficient is marginal. In string compactifications it is often controlled by moduli and threshold data. Thus, deriving gauge couplings requires more than enforcing charge quantization or representation completeness: it requires eliminating the gauge-kinetic modulus.

Quantum gravity is expected to forbid exact global symmetries, require compact gauge groups, and enforce completeness of charge sectors. These principles constrain which line operators and charged states may exist. They do not, by themselves, compute the absolute gauge stiffness. We therefore ask whether there can exist a quantum-gravitational phase in which the gauge kinetic coefficient is not a continuous modulus but a protected response of a microscopic Hilbert space.

We introduce such a framework, called **Irreducible Self-Dual Line-Code Quantum Gravity**. The central hypothesis is that an infrared gauge connection is the Berry connection of a primitive line-code Hilbert space, and that the ultraviolet inverse coupling is the ground-state holonomy stiffness:
[
\alpha_U^{-1}
=============

\left.
\frac{\partial^2E_0(\phi)}{\partial\phi^2}
\right|_{\phi=0}.
]
The line-code phase is assumed to be irreducible, self-dual and without a kinetic modulus. Under these assumptions, the stiffness is not tunable: it is the trace of a primitive line-code stiffness operator.

We then specialize the construction to a one-threshold Pati–Salam chain embedded in (\mathrm{Spin}(10)). The model has three essential ingredients.

First, the ultraviolet coupling is selected by the (D_5) root system and a minimal (T^3) two-form flux clock:
[
\alpha_U^{-1}=40+5+\frac32=\frac{93}{2}.
]

Second, the energy scales are fixed by two discrete clocks. The unification scale is
[
M_U=\bar M_{\rm Pl}/40,
]
and the intermediate Pati–Salam scale is determined by
[
\ln(M_U/M_I):\ln(M_I/M_Z)=6:13.
]

Third, the Pati–Salam running and finite matching are supplied by two distinct non-propagating line-code structures. The **line-code index** (LCI) is a scale-local Wilsonian Jacobian anomaly that changes the logarithmic slope. The **dyadic finite determinant complex** (DFC) is a matching-surface superdeterminant that contributes once. Confusing these with ordinary propagating messenger fields destroys the precision closure.

The result is a compact, falsifiable gauge-sector prediction. The model predicts the weak-scale running quantities
[
\hat\alpha_{\rm em}(M_Z),\qquad
\sin^2\hat\theta_W(M_Z),\qquad

It does not claim to predict the low-energy Thomson-limit value (\alpha(0)). That value differs from (\hat\alpha(M_Z)) by charged-lepton thresholds, quark thresholds, electroweak matching and hadronic vacuum polarization. The latter involves non-perturbative QCD. The present article therefore focuses on the gauge-coupling selection problem only.
